# Air Quality Index (AQI) Classification Scale

The Air Quality Index (AQI) is used to communicate the health risks of ambient air pollution:

- **0 - 50 (Good)**: Air quality is satisfactory, and air pollution poses little or no risk.
- **51 - 100 (Satisfactory)**: Air quality is acceptable. However, there may be a risk for some people, particularly those who are unusually sensitive to air pollution.
- **101 - 200 (Moderate)**: Members of sensitive groups (asthma, heart disease, children) may experience health effects. The general public is less likely to be affected.
- **201 - 300 (Poor)**: Health alert: everyone may begin to experience health effects; members of sensitive groups may experience more serious health effects.
- **301 - 400 (Very Poor)**: Health warnings of emergency conditions. The entire population is more likely to be affected.
- **401+ (Severe / Hazardous)**: Health warning: everyone may experience more serious health effects. Outdoor activity should be strictly restricted.
