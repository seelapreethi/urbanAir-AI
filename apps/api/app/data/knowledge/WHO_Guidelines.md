# WHO Global Air Quality Guidelines

The World Health Organization (WHO) guidelines recommend limits for key air pollutants to protect public health:

1. **PM2.5 (Fine Particulate Matter)**:
   - Annual mean concentration should not exceed 5 µg/m³.
   - 24-hour mean exposure should not exceed 15 µg/m³.
   - PM2.5 can penetrate deep into the lungs and enter the bloodstream, causing cardiovascular and respiratory illness.

2. **PM10 (Coarse Particulate Matter)**:
   - Annual mean concentration should not exceed 15 µg/m³.
   - 24-hour mean exposure should not exceed 45 µg/m³.

3. **Nitrogen Dioxide (NO2)**:
   - Annual mean concentration should not exceed 10 µg/m³.
   - 24-hour mean exposure should not exceed 25 µg/m³.

4. **Ozone (O3)**:
   - 8-hour daily maximum should not exceed 100 µg/m³.

5. **Sulfur Dioxide (SO2)**:
   - 24-hour mean exposure should not exceed 40 µg/m³.
